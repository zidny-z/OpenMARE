### **Goals**
*   **Primary Goal:** To create a simple, engaging mobile app that helps everyday people build healthier habits by tracking their daily activities and progress toward personal health goals.
*   **User Adoption:** I want users to open the app daily and feel motivated, not overwhelmed. It should feel like a supportive companion.
*   **Behavior Change:** The ultimate success is seeing users report that the app genuinely helped them improve their physical activity, sleep, nutrition, or mindfulness.
*   **Foundation for Growth:** I want to build a solid, loved core product first, with the potential to add more advanced features or community aspects later based on user feedback.

### **Needs**
*   **Intuitive Onboarding:** New users must be able to set up their first goal and understand how to log an activity within minutes, without a manual.
*   **Flexible Tracking:** Users need to track different types of activities (e.g., walking, water intake, meditation, sleep) in a way that feels natural for each. A walk is logged differently than a glass of water.
*   **Clear Visual Progress:** Users need to see their progress at a glance—through simple charts, streaks, or achievement badges—to stay motivated.
*   **Personalization:** Users should be able to set their own goals (e.g., "8,000 steps a day," "drink 2 liters of water," "sleep 7 hours") that are meaningful to *them*, not generic targets.
*   **Reliable Reminders & Notifications:** The app should help users build a routine with gentle, customizable reminders to log activities or to get moving.

### **Constraints**
*   **Budget:** Initial development and launch costs must be carefully managed. We need to prioritize core features that deliver the most value.
*   **Time:** We aim to launch a functional first version (a "Minimum Viable Product") within a set timeframe to start getting real user feedback as soon as possible.
*   **Platform Decision:** We need to decide whether to launch on iOS, Android, or both simultaneously, considering our target audience and budget.
*   **Data Privacy:** We must handle user health data with extreme care, be transparent about what we collect, and comply with all relevant regulations (like app store guidelines) from day one.

### **Pain Points (with existing methods or competitors)**
*   **Overwhelm:** Many health apps are too complex, with dozens of metrics that confuse and demotivate the average user. I want to avoid "analysis paralysis."
*   **Lack of Flexibility:** Some apps are too rigid, forcing users into predefined goals or activity types that don't fit their personal health journey.
*   **Poor Motivation:** Simply logging data isn't enough. If the app doesn't make users *feel* good about their progress—even small wins—they will disengage quickly.
*   **High Abandonment Rate:** People often download health apps, use them for a week, and then forget about them. We need to combat this drop-off by making the experience consistently rewarding and low-friction.
*   **Data Silos:** Users often have to use multiple apps (one for steps, one for food, etc.). While we can't do everything at first, I want our app to feel cohesive for the few things it *does* track.

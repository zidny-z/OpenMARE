# Food Ordering System: Stakeholder Overview

## Primary Goals
- Enable customers to easily browse restaurant menus with clear descriptions and pricing
- Provide a seamless ordering process from selection to payment
- Offer real-time order tracking so customers know exactly when their food will arrive
- Increase order accuracy and reduce errors compared to phone orders
- Build customer loyalty through a convenient, reliable ordering experience

## Key Needs
- Customers need to find what they want quickly with intuitive navigation
- Menu information must be current, accurate, and reflect availability
- Payment processing must be secure and support multiple payment methods
- Order status should update automatically at key stages (confirmed, preparing, out for delivery, delivered)
- Customers need clear communication about delivery times and any delays
- The system should accommodate special requests or dietary modifications

## Business Constraints
- Must work across different devices (phones, tablets, computers)
- Needs to integrate with existing restaurant operations and kitchen workflows
- Budget limitations for development and ongoing maintenance
- Timeline constraints for launch
- Must comply with payment card industry standards and data privacy regulations
- Should support multiple restaurants or locations if we expand

## Current Pain Points (with traditional ordering)
- Phone orders often have miscommunications about menu items or special requests
- Customers frequently call to ask "where's my order?" creating operational interruptions
- Manual order entry leads to errors that affect customer satisfaction
- Limited ability to upsell or suggest additional items during phone orders
- Difficulty managing peak order times with limited phone lines
- No easy way for customers to reorder favorite items
- Inconsistent order taking quality across different staff members

## Success Metrics
- Reduced order errors and customer complaints
- Increased order frequency from existing customers
- Higher average order value compared to phone orders
- Decreased staff time spent on order taking and status inquiries
- Positive customer feedback about the ordering experience
